End-Consumer Privacy Policy

This policy explains how we handle your information when you interact with missed call workflows powered by BookedUp AI Systems on behalf of our business clients.

1. Who we are

BookedUp AI Systems ("we", "us") provides AI-powered missed call workflows and related automations to local and small businesses (our "clients"). We operate these workflows on behalf of our clients so they can respond to missed calls, answer questions, and book jobs more efficiently.

2. Who this policy is for

This policy is intended for end consumers who contact one of our clients by phone or SMS and whose interaction is handled in whole or in part by a workflow we provide. Our clients may have their own privacy notices which also apply.

3. Information we collect about you

Depending on how you interact with a client using our workflows, we may process the following information on their behalf:

  • Basic contact details such as your phone number and (if you provide it) your name and email address.
  • Message content from SMS or chat conversations handled by the workflow.
  • Call-related information such as the date and time of the call, call status (answered, missed), and basic technical metadata.
  • Service or booking details you provide, such as the type of job requested, preferred dates, and location.

4. How we use information

We process information on behalf of our clients in order to:

  • Detect missed calls and trigger follow-up messages.
  • Answer common questions about our clients' services, availability, and pricing where appropriate.
  • Collect the information needed to prepare quotes or schedule appointments.
  • Book confirmed appointments or callbacks into our clients' calendars or systems.
  • Provide our clients with summaries and analytics about missed calls and recovered business.

5. Legal basis and role

In most cases, our clients act as the primary data controllers for your information and determine the lawful basis for processing. We act as a service provider or processor, using your information only as necessary to operate the workflows and as instructed by our clients.

6. Data sharing and transfers

We may share or store your information in systems that our clients use, such as their phone system, CRM, calendar, messaging tools, or ticketing software. We may also use reputable third-party providers to host and run our workflow infrastructure. Where we do so, we take steps to help protect your information through access controls and security practices.

7. Data retention

We retain information for as long as is reasonably necessary to operate the workflows for our clients, to maintain accurate records of missed calls and bookings, and to comply with legal or contractual obligations. Our clients may request that we delete or anonymize data earlier in line with their own retention policies.

8. Your rights and choices

Depending on where you live, you may have rights over your personal information, such as the right to access, correct, or delete it, or to object to certain kinds of processing. Because we provide services on behalf of our clients, the most direct way to exercise these rights is usually to contact the business you originally called.

If you contact us directly about a workflow we operate, we may need to refer your request to the relevant client and support them in responding.
